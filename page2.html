AIRCHAMBER

‘Airchamber’ is in fact a thesis work that went out of control. What started out as a final project in secundary school expanded into a threefolded research project which continued for two years.

‘Germinating’, ‘Growing’ & ‘Dying’ are the titles for every component of ‘Airchamber’, for an idea that used to be the theme of my work.

Part I Germinating

‘Germinating’ is the title of the first part of ‘Airchamber’. The vocation of my thesis became the exploration of the unexistant term ‘Airchaber’. ‘Germinating’ in particular focuses on experimenting with different materials. The photograph series which resulted out of the whole working process had a special value to me, so I decided to expand on the work even after the deadline. It appeared to be just the tip of the iceberg.

Part II Growing

‘Growing’ is the component wherein the term ‘air room’ was examined by painting. Compared to ‘Germinating’, this series grew in a similar way: only the pieces which were relevant to the whole work of ‘Airchamber’ were used and expanded upon. The multitude of images coming out of it caused the audience to gradually explore the boundaries of ‘Airchamber’.

Part III Dying

‘Dying’ is just as melancholic as ‘Germinating’ is new, and ‘Growing’ is ambitious. This component puts an end to ‘Airchamber’. Inspired by the ‘vegetation myths’ and by obsessively calling the whole project ‘my child’, the three titles came to life. After this, ‘Airchamber’ was buried. The airchambers change into flat paper and lose their form.
